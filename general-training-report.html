1. Introduction and Context

This report documents the General Training delivered to volunteers selected for the Trail Angels European Solidarity Corps (ESC) Team Volunteering project (No. 101093414). The training was conducted in the preparatory phase prior to the volunteers' arrival in Tuscany, Italy, and was designed to equip participants with the knowledge, skills, and practical information necessary to engage fully and responsibly in their volunteering experience.

The training was organised as an online preparatory programme held via Zoom video conference, in accordance with the project's approved work plan. The programme was co-designed by the host organisation (CNV) and the sending organisation (IB Polska), drawing on the European Solidarity Corps framework, Youthpass competence development approach, and relevant EU policy documents.

The training addressed all six thematic areas identified in the approved project proposal, totalling approximately nine hours of structured learning activity.

4. Description of Training Sessions and Content

Below is a description of the content delivered in each of the six thematic areas covered during the preparatory training programme, in accordance with the approved project proposal.

1
General Principles of the European Union
~1 hour

Participants were introduced to the founding principles and values of the European Union, with a focus on solidarity, active citizenship, cultural diversity, and democratic participation. The session contextualised the ESC programme within the broader EU framework and encouraged volunteers to reflect on their identity as European citizens.

  • EU institutions and their roles (Commission, Parliament, Council)
  • EU citizenship rights and the principle of free movement
  • Values of solidarity and social cohesion underpinning the ESC
  • Volunteers' role as EU ambassadors during the project
Materials used: IB Polska preparatory meeting slide deck (slides 3–4); ESC INFO KIT introductory section.
2
ESC Programme — Requirements, Procedures & Volunteer Rights
~2 hours

This session provided a comprehensive overview of the European Solidarity Corps programme, including the rights and obligations of volunteers, the role of the sending and host organisations, practical procedures for the mobility, and the ESC rules of conduct. The session was facilitated by IB Polska, the sending organisation, who has extensive experience in ESC project management.

  • What is the ESC? Programme objectives and beneficiary categories
  • The roles of host, sending, and coordinating organisations
  • Volunteer agreement and its key clauses
  • ESC INFO KIT rules: pocket money, working hours, accommodation, food, transport
  • Practical procedures: pre-departure checklist, emergency contacts, insurance activation
  • Youthpass overview: what it is and how it will be developed during the project
Materials used: IB Polska slide deck — Trail Angels: Prep-meeting for volunteers selected to the project in 2023 (full deck, 7 slides); ESC INFO KIT (official ESC documentation).
3
Project Activities — Timeline, Logistics, Tasks & Duties
~1.5 hours

Volunteers were briefed on the specific activities they would carry out as part of the Trail Angels project in Tuscany. The session provided clarity on the day-to-day structure of the volunteering experience, the tasks assigned to each volunteer, and the logistical arrangements for their stay in Lucca.

  • Project timeline and key milestones across the volunteering period
  • Description of trail maintenance, signage and conservation tasks
  • Working hours, rest days, and supervision arrangements
  • Accommodation at CNV Lucca: address, facilities, house rules
  • Travel reimbursement procedures
  • Communication channels and emergency protocols
Materials used: CNV project coordination slides; IB Polska prep-meeting deck (session details section).
4
European Cultural Routes — Via Francigena
~45 min

This session introduced volunteers to the Via Francigena as one of Europe's major Cultural Routes, certified by the Council of Europe. Understanding the historical and cultural significance of the route they would be helping to maintain was considered essential for informed and motivated volunteering. Participants explored the route's history from Canterbury to Rome, with particular attention to the Tuscan section passing through Lucca.

  • History of the Via Francigena as a medieval pilgrimage route
  • Council of Europe Cultural Routes certification scheme
  • The route through Tuscany and its UNESCO connections
  • Current state of the trail and the role of volunteer maintenance
Materials used: "Get Out of the Country" workshop deck (cultural routes section); interactive map of Via Francigena in Tuscany.
5
European Policies on Cultural Heritage
~1.5 hours

Participants deepened their understanding of how the European Union approaches the protection and promotion of cultural heritage. The session explored the EU Work Plan for Culture, the European Heritage Label, and the connection between cultural heritage, sustainable tourism, and community volunteering. Case studies from EU-funded heritage projects were presented.

  • EU Framework for Cultural Heritage: key policy documents and instruments
  • European Year of Cultural Heritage 2018 and its legacy
  • European Heritage Label and its significance
  • UNESCO World Heritage Sites and the volunteer role in their preservation
  • Synergies between ESC volunteering and cultural heritage protection
Materials used: "Get Out of the Country" workshop deck; EU Cultural Heritage policy documents (excerpts); Zoom breakout group discussions.
6
European Policies on Environment, Green & Climate Actions
~2 hours

The final thematic session addressed the EU's ambitious environmental agenda and how volunteering activities in Trail Angels connect to broader European Green Deal objectives. Participants explored biodiversity, sustainable land management, and the practical environmental impact of their trail work. The session included interactive exercises asking volunteers to identify green actions in their own lives.

  • European Green Deal: key targets and policy instruments
  • EU Biodiversity Strategy 2030 and its relevance for trail ecosystems
  • Sustainable land use and nature-based solutions in Tuscany
  • Climate change and its impact on rural heritage landscapes
  • Volunteer contribution to green infrastructure maintenance
  • Personal carbon footprint and sustainable travel considerations
Materials used: "Get Out of the Country" workshop deck (environment and climate section); EU Green Deal policy brief; Zoom interactive polls and group exercises.

7. Youthpass Competences Addressed

The training programme was designed in alignment with the Youthpass key competence framework. The following competences were explicitly addressed and form the basis for each volunteer's individual Youthpass certificate:

Citizenship Competence
Understanding EU institutions, solidarity values, ESC programme objectives, and the role of active citizenship.
Cultural Awareness & Expression
Knowledge of the Via Francigena, European cultural heritage policies, and appreciation of diverse cultural traditions.
Personal, Social & Learning to Learn
Self-reflection on learning goals, intercultural communication, conflict management, and collaborative working.
Multilingual Competence
Active participation in English-language training; exposure to multilingual peer interaction across EU member states.
Digital Competence
Use of Zoom, digital collaboration tools, ESC online portal, and Youthpass online platform (youthpass.eu).
Entrepreneurship Competence
Initiative-taking, problem-solving in trail management contexts, and contributing to community-driven environmental projects.

Each volunteer will receive an individual Youthpass certificate upon successful completion of their volunteering period, documenting their personal competence development in relation to the learning objectives set during this preparatory training.

9. Training Outcomes and Evaluation

At the end of the training programme, volunteers were invited to complete a short evaluation questionnaire. The feedback was overwhelmingly positive, with participants noting the clarity of information provided on ESC procedures, the engaging workshop format, and the value of meeting fellow volunteers before arrival. Key outcomes included:

  • All participants completed the preparatory training and confirmed receipt of the insurance guide and Youthpass information.
  • Volunteers demonstrated understanding of ESC rules, rights, and obligations through post-session Q&A.
  • Each participant set personal learning objectives in the Youthpass framework to be developed during the project.
  • Strong peer community was formed among volunteers prior to arrival, supporting social cohesion during the project.
  • Volunteers expressed increased awareness of EU cultural heritage and environmental policy and its connection to their volunteering tasks.
