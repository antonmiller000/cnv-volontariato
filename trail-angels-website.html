About the Project
A month in the heart of Tuscany — for nature, for people, for Europe.

Trail Angels is a one-month ESC volunteering project taking place in the rural areas of Tuscany, Italy. International teams of young people live and work together to maintain and improve hiking trails used by locals, pilgrims, and tourists every year.

The project combines environmental conservation, cultural exchange, and community engagement. Volunteers work side by side to restore paths, support pilgrims on ancient routes, and bring life to local communities — all while developing personal skills and gaining a unique international experience.

The work is physical and outdoors, but deeply rewarding. Volunteers literally make nature more accessible for everyone, living surrounded by mountains, forests, and the iconic Tuscan landscape.

The project is organised by CNV Lucca (Centro Nazionale Per Il Volontariato), one of Italy's leading volunteering organisations with over 30 years of experience in civic engagement, ESC and Erasmus+ youth mobility programmes.

What Volunteers Do
Every day brings a mix of outdoor work, community connection, and personal growth.
🪓

Trail Maintenance & Marking

Clearing vegetation, repairing damaged paths, installing trail markers, building small structures, and cleaning natural areas to keep routes safe and accessible.

🧭

Supporting Hikers & Pilgrims

Providing assistance and information to walkers on the Cammino del Volto Santo and other historic local pilgrim routes through Tuscany.

🌿

Environmental Actions

Participating in biodiversity protection initiatives, sustainable tourism awareness campaigns, and ecological restoration in natural parks across the region.

🎨

Community Events & Workshops

Organising cultural exchanges, creative workshops, and small community events that bring locals and volunteers together, celebrating diversity and European solidarity.

🍳

Communal Life

Sharing daily responsibilities — cooking meals together, maintaining shared spaces, and building the kind of intercultural friendship that lasts a lifetime.

📱

Social Media & Documentation

Capturing and sharing the project story through photos, videos, and social media — giving visibility to the ESC programme and the volunteer team's work.

Volunteering Periods & Locations
Three distinct periods across some of Tuscany's most beautiful territories. Exact dates may vary slightly — confirmed upon selection.
Period 1
01 May – 30 May 2026
📍 Castelnuovo di Garfagnana

Nestled in the Garfagnana mountains of northern Tuscany, this medieval town sits between the Apuan Alps and the Apennines. Volunteers work on mountain trails surrounded by chestnut forests and crystal-clear rivers in one of Italy's most spectacular yet undiscovered natural areas.

Period 2
01 June – 30 June 2026
📍 La Brilla – Porta del Parco

Located in the Massarosa area, La Brilla serves as a gateway to the Migliarino-San Rossore-Massaciuccoli Regional Park. Volunteers work at the interface between nature reserves, lake ecosystems, and Mediterranean coastal forests.

Period 3
01 July – 30 July 2026
📍 Fucecchio

Fucecchio is home to one of the largest inland wetlands in Italy. Volunteers contribute to trail and nature management in this extraordinary wetland ecosystem, rich in birdlife and biodiversity, close to Florence and Pisa. Accommodation for this period is provided at Ostello Ponte dè Medici and its dependance Villa Ruben, both located in Fucecchio (FI).

Key Trails & Natural Areas Maintained

Cammino del Volto Santo — A historic pilgrim route connecting Lucca to the Garfagnana mountains. Trail Angels volunteers provide active support and maintenance on this ancient path, used by thousands of pilgrims and hikers annually.

Garfagnana Mountain Trails — Mountain paths through the Apuan Alps and Apennine ridges near Castelnuovo di Garfagnana, maintained and marked by volunteers during the May period.

Migliarino-San Rossore-Massaciuccoli Regional Park — One of Tuscany's most important protected areas. June volunteers work on ecological trails connecting lake ecosystems and coastal pinewood forests.

Fucecchio Marshes (Padule di Fucecchio) — The largest inland wetland in Italy. July volunteers maintain access paths and support biodiversity conservation in this internationally recognised nature reserve.

Our International Volunteer Team
Trail Angels brings together young people from across Europe and beyond.

Each volunteering group is made up of young people from different countries, backgrounds, and life experiences — united by a shared desire to contribute to something meaningful. The international team structure is a core learning experience in itself, promoting intercultural dialogue, cooperation, and European solidarity in practice.

Who Can Apply
Trail Angels welcomes motivated young Europeans — no prior experience needed.
  • ✓ Young people aged 18 to 30 years old
  • ✓ Motivated, open-minded, ready to live and work in an international team
  • ✓ No previous volunteering experience required
  • ✓ EU nationalities (or countries with ESC eligibility agreements)
  • ✓ Comfortable with shared accommodation and communal living
  • ✓ Ready for physical, outdoor work in all weather conditions
  • ✓ Basic English communication skills (B1 level recommended)

How to Apply

  1. Complete the online application form with your personal details and motivation
  2. Book a 20-minute online interview slot via the provided Calendly link
  3. Receive a confirmation and project InfoPack with all practical details
  4. Register on the ESC portal and obtain your Personal Reference Number

For questions, contact CNV Lucca at trailangels@cnvlucca.it
An InfoPack with accommodation photos, daily schedule, and packing list is available upon request.

European Solidarity Corps – Funding Acknowledgement & Disclaimer

Trail Angels (Project No. 101093414) is funded by the European Union through the European Solidarity Corps (ESC) Programme, managed by the European Education and Culture Executive Agency (EACEA) on behalf of the European Commission.

The European Solidarity Corps is the European Union's initiative for young people who want to volunteer or work in projects that benefit communities and people throughout Europe. Through ESC, the EU invests in young people's personal development, intercultural competences, and active citizenship.
